ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Artificial Intelligence (AI) represents a paradigm in computer science and technology that seeks to imbue machines with the ability to perform tasks that traditionally required human intelligence. The goal is to create systems capable of learning, reasoning, problem-solving, understanding natural language, and adapting to diverse situations. AI encompasses a spectrum of techniques and approaches, ranging from rule-based systems to advanced machine learning and deep learning algorithms.

The concept of AI has deep historical roots, with early philosophical and mythological inquiries into the nature of intelligence. However, the modern pursuit of AI as a scientific discipline began in the mid-20th century. In 1955, John McCarthy, along with Marvin Minsky, Nathaniel Rochester, and Claude Shannon, organized the Dartmouth Conference, where the term "artificial intelligence" was coined. This marked the formal beginning of AI as an academic and research field.

AUTONOMOUS CAR

A self-driving car (sometimes called an autonomous car or driverless car) is a vehicle that uses a combination of sensors, cameras, radar and artificial intelligence (AI) to travel between destinations without a human operator.
